What is stitching?

Stitching is the method of joining fabrics or materials using needle and thread. From repairing seams to decorative embroidery, hand stitching is versatile, portable, and deeply satisfying.

Why stitch by hand?

  • Precision and control for delicate work
  • Portable—no machine required
  • Mindful, relaxing craft

Where it’s used

  • Clothing repairs and alterations
  • Quilting, embroidery, and crafts
  • Leatherwork and bookbinding

Key skills

  • Threading and knotting
  • Even spacing and tension
  • Knowing the right stitch for the job

Tools and materials

Start with a simple kit. Upgrade as your projects grow.

🪡

Needles

Sharps for general use, chenille for embroidery, leather needles for heavy materials.

🧵

Thread

Polyester or cotton for fabric, waxed thread for leather. Match weight to material.

✂️

Scissors

Fabric shears and small snips keep cuts clean and precise.

📏

Measuring

Tape, ruler, and chalk or erasable marker for marking lines and spacing.

🧽

Thimble

Protects your finger and improves pushing power through tough fabrics.

🕯️

Wax

Lightly waxing thread reduces tangles and strengthens stitches.

Basic techniques

Follow these steps to set yourself up for clean, durable seams.

  1. Thread the needle

    Cut thread at an angle. Wet the tip if needed. Insert through the eye and pull 30–45 cm.

  2. Knot the end

    Use a simple overhand knot or quilter’s knot to secure the thread.

  3. Mark your seam

    Lightly mark the seam line and spacing with chalk. Use a ruler or spacing wheel.

  4. Maintain tension

    Keep stitches even—snug but not puckering. Relax shoulders and hands.

  5. Finish and secure

    Backstitch at the end or tie a small knot and bury the tail between layers.

Common stitches

Each stitch has a purpose. Learn when and why to use them.

Running stitch

Simple in‑out motion for basting and light seams.

Backstitch

Strong, machine‑like seam for durable joins and repairs.

Blanket/whip stitch

Secures raw edges on blankets, felt, and leather.

Slip/ladder stitch

Invisible seam for closing openings by hand.

FAQ

Quick answers for common beginner questions.

How do I keep my stitches even?

Mark guide lines, use a thimble for consistent pushing, and check spacing every few stitches.

Why does my thread tangle?

Use shorter lengths (no more than forearm), condition with wax, and let the needle dangle to remove twist.

Which thread should I buy first?

Start with all‑purpose polyester or cotton in neutral colors; add heavier waxed thread for leather later.
